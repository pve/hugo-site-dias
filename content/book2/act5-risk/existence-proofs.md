## Thesis

After the risks (79), the autarky illusion (80), and the controls (81), the reader's fair question is: does any of this actually work?
Yes — there are running, decades-old proofs that shared digital infrastructure can be built outside big tech, and their common pattern is learnable.

## Position in the arc

Between autonomy controls (81) and the organising-markets chapter (82): this unit supplies the evidence; 82 extracts the mechanism.
Keep the split clean: here the stories, there the theory of demand aggregation.

## The proofs (one tight page each, at most)

- **eduroam / EDUVPN**: global federated wifi grown out of the education sector; a student's credential works on another continent.
  Double payoff: it is also the federation callback to the identity unit (11a) — autonomy achieved by federating trust, not by centralizing it.
- **SURF / GÉANT**: standing cooperative digital infrastructure for research and education; the €230k digital-research-environment grant (SURF newsletter link in snippets.txt) as a small, current example of how it keeps building.
- **Haven / Common Ground (VNG)**: ~340 Dutch municipalities aggregating demand; already referenced in digital-autonomy-controls — here it gets its story told rather than cited.
- **India UPI**: the non-European, non-niche proof: open mandated API standard, 550+ banks, hundreds of millions of users; broke bank lock-in at national scale.
- **UK G-Cloud**: deliberately kept as the double-edged one: SMEs won ~56% of spend (2013), ~£3bn cumulative by 2018 — then AWS/Microsoft opened UK datacentres (2016) and reclaimed the framework.
  Lesson: open access alone does not hold incumbents back; demand must be continuously shaped (hand-off to 82).
- **SCS / Gaia-X**: honest cautionary contrast — the right idea moving at the wrong speed; name why (governance weight before user value).

## The pattern to extract (closing section)

Successes share three things: a standing demand aggregator, an open standard at the interface, and governance that actively keeps the market open over time.
Failures are missing at least one.
Do not oversell: none of these removed dependence; they changed its terms — which is exactly the book's definition of autonomy (80).
