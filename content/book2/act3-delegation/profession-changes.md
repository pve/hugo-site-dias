## Thesis

In fifty-plus years of watching software development, nothing has changed the profession deeper or faster than AI coding (harvest the opening claim of ai-coding-age — it is strong, keep it).
But what changes is *which* work is delegated: AI replaces coders, not software engineers — the mechanical translation of intent into code goes first; owning the intent, the architecture, and the promises to users does not.

## Position in the arc

The act has shown machines taking over ever more (DHCP → feedback loops → AI systems → agents).
This unit brings it home: the delegation now reaches the reader's own profession — including the professions of Neil and Maria, not just programmers.

## What goes in

- The coder vs software engineer distinction (ai-code-software): a coder translates specifications into code; an engineer decides what to build, verifies it works, and answers for it.
  Delegation takes the first, sharpens the need for the second.
- The reality check (vibe-coding-real): the experienced SaaS-founder friend's account — what vibe coding actually does well, where it fails, what discipline it takes.
  Keep his practitioner voice; it inoculates against both hype and dismissal.
- The author's own experiment arc, compressed to a paragraph each (full versions feed unit 46):
  tic-tac-toe without looking at a line of code (ai-nocode, "Look Ma, no hands"); the rabbit-holes experiment (monkey-coding); the first real production app (real-app-with-ai).
  The lesson curve: it works → it works only with discipline → the discipline *is* the job.
- What the discipline consists of (bridge to promise language): specifying intent, bounding the agent (sandboxes, constraints — vibe-deploy), verifying outcomes, keeping the feedback loop (unit 36, OHDA) closed.
- "Orchestrator instead of minion" — deliberately echo unit 34's phrase; the emotional payoff of delegation done right.
- Widen beyond coding in the close: risk assessments, compliance checks, architecture documents are next (link ai-risk-classification / risk-value material in unit 74); every knowledge profession gets its own version of this unit's story.
