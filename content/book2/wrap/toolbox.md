## Purpose

The preface promises "in these writings I try to open up my mental toolbox."
This appendix makes that literal: five one-pagers, one per act, each usable without rereading the book — printable, meeting-ready.
Neil carries them into meetings; Maria tapes them next to her diagram.

## Format rules

- One page per act, hard limit.
- Questions and steps, not theory; every item points back to the unit where it is argued.
- Each page usable standalone: a colleague who never read the book should be able to apply it.

## Page 1 — Act I: Read any infrastructure

- The boundary-promise walkthrough (the Maria exercise, units 14/16/17): draw the boxes, draw the arrows, and per arrow name: the promise, the promiser, the identity it runs under (11a), and what happens when the promise is broken.
- The infrastructure test (unit 5): independent of a single use, provisioned on a longer timescale, not owned by its users.

## Page 2 — Act II: Price the deal

- The value questions (units 20–22): where does this create value elsewhere, for whom, and how would we notice?
- The automation business-case check (unit 37, xkcd 1205): time saved × frequency vs cost of automating.
- The switching-cost worksheet (unit 32a): per critical interface — what would it cost to leave, in money, months, and morale?
  If you don't know the number, the provider does.

## Page 3 — Act III: Delegate without regret

- The four-question agent test (unit 44): the promise, the boundary, the principal, the verification.
- The delegation checklist (units 36/39/45): is the feedback loop closed, who reviews the outcomes, what may this agent never do, and is there a kill switch that actually works?
- OHDA (unit 36) as the troubleshooting loop: Observe, Hypothesize, Decide, Act.

## Page 4 — Act IV: Map the power

- The power-map questions (units 49–56): who decides, who mediates (52), who suffers (65 — deliberately also on page 5), where does the buck stop (54), and who has power over the asset that matters (32a's pizzeria question)?
- The disruption check (57): what would have to tip for this arrangement to flip?

## Page 5 — Act V: Stay in control

- The Kipling method questions (unit 76): who, what, when, where, why, how of every allowed communication.
- The DaRiCo starter (69) and the who-suffers question (65) as the first two moves of any risk conversation.
- The lethal-trifecta test for agents (75): private data, untrusted content, exfiltration channel — remove one.
- The deterrence checklist (81/83): a costed, rehearsed exit option per critical dependency; an exit that isn't credible deters no one.
